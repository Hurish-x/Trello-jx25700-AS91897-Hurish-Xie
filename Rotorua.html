Dive Deeper into each destination

Te Puia

Te Puia is one of Rotorua’s most famous geothermal parks, known for the powerful Pohutu Geyser and bubbling mud pools. It also offers Māori cultural experiences and traditional crafts.

Read more

Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland

Wai-O-Tapu is famous for its colourful geothermal features, including the iconic Champagne Pool and bright green lakes. It is one of the most visually unique places in Rotorua.

Read more

Waimangu Volcanic Valley

Waimangu is the world’s youngest geothermal valley, formed after a volcanic eruption. It features large hot springs, craters, and unique natural landscapes.

Read more

Redwoods Whakarewarewa Forest

This forest is known for its tall redwood trees and peaceful walking tracks. It is a great place for nature walks, biking, and scenic photography.

Read more

Polynesian Spa

Polynesian Spa is a popular place to relax in natural hot pools. It offers beautiful lake views and a calming experience after exploring Rotorua.
